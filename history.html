The Sea Peoples

Around 1210 BCE appeared a nation of seafarers seemingly out of nowhere, there are many theories about their origin but they remain unidentified.
They attacked Egypt and other regions around the East Mediterranean such as Cyprus, Syria, Anatolia. The discovery of the reports about them around the 19th
is one of the most famous chapters of Egyptian history.
The Egyptians have written about them on many steles and reliefs, starting with Ramesses II.
Some strange detail about this piece of history is that we know that the nations around the Mediterranean knew each other very well,
and they could identify other peoples through their clothes and weapons. But even reporters at that period in time had no idea about the identity
of those invaders. Drawings on many walls show their particular pieces of armor, and their style remains unidentified until today.
Another detail is that it seems like those Sea Peoples disappeared as abruptly as they first appeared, and no traces of them can be found
except for the aformentioned inscriptions and reports.

This subject is dear to me because it shows how little we actually know of history, and how much has been lost. How many books and encyclopedias
could be filled with all this lost information, perhaps never to be recovered again, it boggles the mind.
